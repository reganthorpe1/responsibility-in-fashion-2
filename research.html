Research

Primary and Secondary research on Responsibility in Fashion helped me further clarify the best solution for the problem.

Responsibility in Fashions mission statement is that they are “a nonprofit bringing together sustainable, conscious, ethical, circular, and eco fashion organizations and government programs toward the goal of energizing and bringing innovation to the growing movement toward clean, safe and ethical fashion. They seek to accomplish their mission through creating awareness about fast fashion and getting involved in events. Responsibility in Fashion is located both in New York City and Los Angelos.

Shop Responsibly: The tagline “Demand drives supply” There are four main points: Shop responsibly-made brands Avoid conventional cotton Avoid conventional leather Avoid conventional dry cleaning

Get Involved: There are buttons to “volunteer” and “talk to us about corporate sponsorship.” Both of these lead to an automatic email window pop-up. The other button is to “donate,” which leads to a PayPal link. There is also a box to “subscribe to our newsletter.”

The Ugly Truth About Fashion: This section talks about the effects of fast fashion on water and air pollution, greenhouse gas emissions, waste toxic chemical dumping, pesticide and herbicide overuse, poverty wages, hazardous working conditions, worker exploitation, pesticide poisoning, human trafficking, and child labor.

The Chemicals in our Clothes: Alkylphenols, phthalates, brominated and chlorinated flame retardants, azo dyes, organotin compounds, perflourinated chemicals, chlorobenzenes, chlorinated solvents, chlorophenols, short-chain chlorinated paraffins, and heavy metals such as cadmium, lead, mercury, and chromium 6. -I observed Remake, which is another non-profit dedicated to creating awareness on fashion sustainability, which I believe has a successful website solution. On the very front of the webpage is a large image which reads, “Together let’s change fashion” with a large “donate” button. Viewers can see at a first scan of the webpage what their mission is and what action step to take. The bar at the top is very clean and straightforward, helping viewers find exactly what they’re looking for without overwhelming them with information. The web design is simply but functional, and is also visually appealing and modern, which speaks to their millennial audience. I also looked at Labour behind the Label. Their strong branding and clear action steps are some of what make this site successful. The website seems to follow a grid that keeps all information concise and organized. Images are meaningful and apply to the message they are trying to convey. Icons are used to send visual information.
